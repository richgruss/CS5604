Barry: Eeleckshun Day wasn’t a total loss fer t'DemokratsPublished November 5, 2014 | By T' Neck Hizzelf 
	It wuz a greet nite fer Demokrats – un t'issues. 
	Shure, Demokratic can'idates gut shellacket, clobberet, whupd, wallopet – pick yer metafer – n' Tuesdee’s eeleckshun. But voters also passet judgment un dozens o'ballot meesures, an' t'noose thar wuz much bett'r. Un issues at Demokrats tradityunallee champion – minimum wage, gun safetee, abershun rights, votin rights, environmantal perteckshun, paid sick leeve an' criminal justus referm – thay kum out un top.
	Voters n' fer states at wint wit Mitt Romnee n' 2012 – Alaska, Arkansas, Nebraskie an' South Dakota – appervet minimum-wage increeses. At should hep make it easi'r fer Republikuns ta suppert a modest minimum-wage hike n' t'nex Congress. N' Sun Francisco an' Oaklan', ballot meesures ta graduallee increese t'minimum wage ta $15 un hour passet by wide margins, givin Demokrats a futher boost.

Voters n' Washington state appervet a meesure rekwiryun' all gun sales ta be accompaniet by background chex, a law at t'Demokratic-let Senate failt ta pass n' t'wake o't' shootin massacre n' Newtown, Cunectikut. Washington voters also defeetid a competyun' amendment, pushd by t'gun-rights organizatyuns, which aimet ta prevent mer chex. Polleeun' shows at background chex have broad suppert across partees an' gun owners, an' t'Washington referendum may embolte uther states ta take simlar acshun.
	N' addishun, Koloradie’s Demokratic governer, John Hickenloop'r, who pushd fer an' won universal background chex an' limits un high-capacitee magazines, appeers ta have eket out a victery despite steddy attax frum t'Nashshunal Rifle Associashun an' uther gun-rights groups.
	Un abershun, voters n' Koloradie an' Nerth Dakota rejectid state constitutyunal amendmants at definet humun life as cummencin at concepshun – a direck challenge ta Roe v. Wade. T' Koloradie meesure would have changd its criminal code ta treet t'unbern as youngns. A ballot meesure n' Tennessee grantyun' t'state legislature explicit autheritee ta regulate abershun passet, but t'state will have ta stay within t'bounds o'Roe.

	Voters n' Cunectikut an' Missouri rejectid proposals ta alloe earlee votin, but t'Missouri defeet wuz a victery fer votin-rights advocates who saw it as a sham. (Proponants o't' Cunectikut meesure could nairy have chose less faverabull werdyun' fer t'ballot.) Illinois voters passet a largelee symbolic votin-rights amendment ta thar state constitushun, but t'mer impertant ballot-access test kum n' Montana, whar voters defeetid a proposal ta end same-day vot'r registrashun.
	Ballot meesures ta protect t'environment also faret well. Voters n' Flerida adoptid a constitutyunal amendment designet ta increese wat'r an' lan' conservashun. Louisyina voters suppertid a fund fer artifishul reef development. N' Californy an' Rhode Islan', voters passet bond meesures a'lettin t'state ta berroe un behalf o'environmantal perteckshun, an' a simlar meesure appeers hedded fer victery n' Mune. New Jersy voters passet a constitutyunal amendment ta protect ope space. Thems victries mer thun made up fer losses n' Nerth Dakota (directin earl revenue ta conservashun) an' Massachusetts (expan'yun' bottle deposits).

	T' Massachusetts ballot also includet a meesure rekwiryun' sartin private secter employers ta provide paid sick leeve ta thar wurkers – un issue at is gunyun' tracshun amungst librul Demokrats. It passet an' could becum a majer issue fer Demokrats n' t'2016 presidenshul eeleckshun. Ferm'r Presdint Bill Clintun sined t'Fambly an' Medicull Leeve Ack into law n' 1993, grantyun' wurkers t'rite ta take time off without losin thar jobs. T' librul wyun' o't' Demokratic Partee could push Hillree Clintun ta embrace paid sick leeve, if'n she runs fer presdint.
	Finallee, voters n' Californy passet a meesure downgradyun' variyus nonviolent crimes ta misdemeeners, rath'r thun felonies, n' un effurt ta reduce incarcerashun. Voters n' Illinois enshrinet a Crime Victim’s Bill o'Rights n' thar constitushun.
	Voters n' Alaska, Oregon an' Washington, D.C., appervet meesures legalizyun' marywanna, un issue at Demokrats tend ta embrace mer thun eagerlee thun Republikuns. Voters n' Oregon passet a gend'r-ekwalitee amendment ta thar state constitushun. And voters n' Missouri rejectid a meesure ta bun teechur tenure an' rekwire teechur evaluatyuns – a big win fer t'eechers’ union, a tradityunal Demokratic allee.
	Republikuns had a numb'r o'impertant ballot meesure victries, includin tax caps n' Geergia an' Tennessee. But un t'whole, eve as t'Demokratic Partee gut un old-fashyunet whuppin’, tradityunal Demokratic issues had un awfullee good nite.
